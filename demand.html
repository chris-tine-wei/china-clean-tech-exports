The reason

Why Europe needs the batteries

Solar is strongest at noon. People use the most power in the evening. Batteries store the extra midday sun and give it back after dark, so gas plants can stay off. Drag through one day to watch it happen.

The bigger picture

One dependence traded for another

For years, Europe's biggest energy weakness was Russian gas. In late 2025 the EU agreed to cut it off by 2027. The replacement is a grid built on wind, solar, and batteries. A lot of that hardware comes from one place: China.

The trade shows it. Europe put tariffs of up to 35% on Chinese electric cars. But the batteries that run its grid come in with no such tax, and those imports jumped about 51% in 2025. It is a new kind of dependence. Cheaper, cleaner, spread across millions of cells. But a dependence all the same.
