The spike

Then, in one month, Africa tripled

In March 2026, China's solar exports to Africa hit $685M — more than triple the month before. Nothing leads up to it. The line just jumps.

Why it happened

Two things lit the fuse

A war in the Middle East sent fuel prices soaring. Countries that import their energy raced to build solar instead.

And China's 9% tax break on solar exports was ending April 1 — so buyers rushed to beat the deadline.

The surge was led by Nigeria, Kenya and Ethiopia, each taking close to a gigawatt of panels in a single month.

Gulf Goes Dark

As Africa lit up, the Gulf went dark

Middle East solar imports fell from a $286M peak to $93M in seven months.

The drop tracks the Strait of Hormuz shutdown in early 2026, when Gulf shipping seized up and cargoes were rerouted.

The bigger picture

Solar is the small story

By dollar value, panels are dwarfed by what stores and moves the power. China ships 2–4× more batteries and electric cars than solar each month.
