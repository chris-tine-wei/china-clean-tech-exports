China's clean-tech exports,
by the gigawatt

China makes ~80% of the world's solar panels and battery cells, and ~70% of its electric cars. Every month, that capacity ships outward. This is where it goes, and how the map redrew itself in 2026.

The exporter

One country, almost every panel

These glowing arcs trace Chinese solar PV exports to their destinations over the last 12 months. The Netherlands (Europe's import gateway), India, Indonesia and the Philippines pull the most.

Arcs are sized by export value. At about $0.10 a watt, $100M of panels is roughly 1 GW, so the dollars and gigawatts ahead are one story in two units.

No single rival appears on this map: the flows all originate from one place.

The 2026 surge

Africa lights up

In March 2026, Chinese solar exports to Africa jumped to $685M in a single month, more than triple February.

The surge was led by Nigeria ($98M), Kenya ($94M) and Ethiopia ($67M), each taking close to a gigawatt of panels in a single month at prevailing prices.

Why now

Two forces behind the rush

The US–Israel war with Iran spiked fossil fuel prices worldwide, pushing the countries most exposed to energy costs to sprint toward solar.

At the same time, China moved to scrap its 9% VAT export rebate on April 1, 2026, triggering a global front-loading rush to lock in cheaper panels before the deadline.

The disruption

The Gulf goes dark

As Africa surged, the Middle East collapsed. Solar imports fell from a $286M peak (Sept 2025) to $93M by April 2026.

The timing tracks the Strait of Hormuz shutdown from late February 2026, when shipping through the Gulf seized up and cargoes were diverted.

The bigger shift

Solar grows, but dollars move to batteries & EVs

Solar's volume keeps climbing, yet in value it's now the small story. Each month China ships 2–4× more batteries and EVs by dollar value than solar PV.

The export engine of the energy transition is shifting from panels to what stores and moves the power.
